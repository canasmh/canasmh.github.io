About me

Hello, my name is Manny and I am a second-year graduate student at New Mexico State University . I am originally from Colombia, but I have spent most of my life in the United States. In May 2018, I received a B.S. in Astrophysics and a B.S. in Physics from the College of Charleston, Charleston, SC. While at College of Charleston, I worked under the mentorship of Dr. George Chartas, using x-ray data to study the powerful outflows that are often seen in active galactic nuclei (AGN).

After graduating from College of Charleston, I moved to Jackson Hole, Wyoming, to work as a stargazing leader for a 501c non-profit organization called Wyoming Stargazing. The company specializes in giving educational and entertaining tours of Jackson-Hole's breath-taking night sky. As such, I became familiar with many of the objects in our night sky, including those that are invisible to the naked eye. Now, I am at New Mexico State University pursuing a Ph.D. in Astronomy. I work under the mentorship of Dr. Wladimir Lyra, using hydrodynamical simulations to study a possible formation scenario for the Kuiper belt.
