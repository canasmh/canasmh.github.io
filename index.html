About me

Hello, my name is Manny and I am a second-year graduate student in the Department of Astronomy at New Mexico State University . I am currently working under the mentorship of Dr. Wladimir Lyra, using hydrodynamical simulations to model the formation of the Kuiper belt. I obtained a B.S. in Astrophysics and a B.S. in Physics from the College of Charleston, Charleston, SC.

Curriculum Vitae

Research Interests

My research involves using hydrodynamical simulations to simulate various planet and planetesimal formation scenarios. I am currently working on a project with Dr. Wladimir Lyra regarding the formation of the Kuiper belt. Our hypothesis is that the strange density distribution of Kuiper belt objects is due to preferential streaming instability of ices and silicates.

Teaching

I have several years of experiences as a teacher assistant for introductory astronomy courses at New Mexico State University and at the College of Charleston. Information regarding the ASTR105 introductory course at NMSU can be found here.

Outreach

I have had extensive experience in outreach through my role as a student of astronomy, and as my brief time as an employee of Wyoming Stargazing, a 501(c)3 non-profit organization specializing in night-sky tours. I have independently led several public stargazing tours, and have helped in events such as the Apache Point Observatory open house.
